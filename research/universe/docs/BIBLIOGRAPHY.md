# 📚 Black Hole Universe — Bibliography

> 50+ references from ancient wisdom to modern AI research

## Ancient & Classical (500 BCE - 1700 CE)

### Pythagoras (~570-495 BCE)
- **"All is number"** — foundational idea that reality is mathematical
- Source: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Pythagoras"

### Plato (~428-348 BCE)
- **Theory of Forms** — archetypes vs shadows
- Source: "Republic", Book VII (Allegory of the Cave)
- Relevance: Files as "shadows" of mathematical "Forms" (seeds)

### Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
- **Potentiality vs Actuality** — a seed is a tree "in potential"
- Source: "Metaphysics", Book IX
- Relevance: A seed is the file "in potential" state

### Fibonacci (Leonardo of Pisa, ~1170-1250)
- **Liber Abaci (1202)** — introduced recursive sequences
- Relevance: Files generated by recurrence rules

### Leibniz (1646-1716)
- **Dissertatio de Arte Combinatoria (1666)** — universal language
- **Characteristica Universalis** — dreamed of a calculus of ideas
- Source: "Philosophical Essays" (1695)
- Relevance: Modern program synthesis as realization

### Euler (1707-1783)
- **Introductio in Analysin Infinitorum (1748)** — mathematical structure
- **Euler's identity**: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$
- Relevance: Minimal structure, maximal connectivity (SIREN inspiration)

## Modern Mathematics (1700-1950)

### Cantor (1845-1918)
- **"Über eine Eigenschaft des Inbegriffes aller reellen algebraischen Zahlen" (1874)**
- Set theory, hierarchy of infinities
- Relevance: Compressible files are countable subset of uncountable file space

### Hilbert (1862-1943)
- **Hilbert's program** (1920s) — formalize all mathematics
- **Entscheidungsproblem** — decision problem
- Relevance: Inspired Turing's work on computability

### Gödel (1906-1978)
- **"Über formal unentscheidbare Sätze" (1931)**
- Incompleteness theorems
- Relevance: Limits of formal systems (related to Chaitin's work)

### Turing (1912-1954)
- **"On Computable Numbers" (1936)**
- Universal Turing Machine
- Relevance: The "universe" is a universal machine; files are programs

### von Neumann (1903-1957)
- **"Theory of Self-Reproducing Automata" (1948, published 1966)**
- Cellular automata
- Relevance: Simple rules generate complex files

---

## Information Theory (1948-1970)

### Shannon (1916-2001)
- **"A Mathematical Theory of Communication" (1948)**
- Information entropy, source coding theorem
- Relevance: Theoretical limit for statistical compression

### Kolmogorov (1903-1987)
- **"Three approaches to the quantitative definition of information" (1965)**
- Algorithmic complexity $K(x)$
- Relevance: The "singularity" — minimum program size

### Solomonoff (1926-2009)
- **"A Preliminary Report on a General Theory of Inductive Inference" (1960)**
- **"A Formal Theory of Inductive Inference" (1964)**
- Universal prior, algorithmic probability
- Relevance: The "multiverse" of programs

### Chaitin (b. 1947)
- **"On the Simplicity and Speed of Programs" (1966)**
- **"Algorithmic Information Theory" (1987)**
- Algorithmic incompleteness
- Relevance: Cannot prove optimality of seeds

### Levin (b. 1948)
- **"Universal Search" (1973)**
- Universal search algorithm
- Relevance: Systematic (if impractical) seed search

## Physics & Holography (1970-2000)

### Bekenstein (1947-2015)
- **"Black holes and entropy" (1973)**
- Bekenstein bound
- Relevance: Physical limits on information density

### Hawking (1942-2018)
- **"Black hole explosions?" (1974)**
- Hawking radiation, black hole thermodynamics
- Relevance: Information paradox — relevant to "information in seeds"

### 't Hooft (b. 1946)
- **"Dimensional reduction in quantum gravity" (1993)**
- Holographic principle (with Susskind)
- Relevance: Boundary encoding of bulk information

### Susskind (b. 1940)
- **"The World as a Hologram" (1994)**
- Holographic principle
- Relevance: Seed = boundary; file = bulk

### Maldacena (b. 1968)
- **"The Large N Limit of Superconformal Field Theories" (1997)**
- AdS/CFT correspondence
- Relevance: Duality between "bulk" (file) and "boundary" (seed)

### Bousso (b. 1971)
- **"A covariant entropy conjecture" (1999)**
- Covariant entropy bound
- Relevance: Tighter physical limits

- Relevance: Physical bounds on compression

- Relevance: Early vision of universe as computation

## Fractal & Iterative Compression (1980-2000)

- Relevance: Baseline compression knowledge

- **"A Universal Algorithm for Sequential Data Compression" (LZ77, 1977)**

- Relevance: Foundational lossless compression

- **"A Block-sorting Lossless Data Compression Algorithm" (BWT, 1994)**
- Relevance: Block transform compression (basis for bzip2)
